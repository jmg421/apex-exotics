## Key Insights

### Price Increases Are Powerful
- Small price increases require relatively small volume losses to break even
- Higher contribution margins make price increases even more attractive
- Example: 5% price increase with 40% margin = can lose 11% volume

### Price Decreases Are Dangerous
- Price cuts require massive volume increases to justify
- Lower contribution margins make discounting extremely risky
- Example: 10% price decrease with 30% margin = need 50% more volume

### Strategic Implications
1. **Defend pricing** - It's usually better to lose some volume than cut prices
2. **Focus on value** - Justify higher prices rather than compete on price
3. **Know your margins** - Higher margins give more pricing flexibility
4. **Volume math is brutal** - Discounting rarely pays off unless you can guarantee massive volume increases

---

## Formula

### Price Increase Break-Even Volume Change:
```
Volume Change % = -Price Increase % / (Contribution Margin % + Price Increase %)
```

### Price Decrease Break-Even Volume Change:
```
Volume Change % = -Price Decrease % / (Contribution Margin % + Price Decrease %)
```

## Application to College Costs

### Scenario: Evaluating "Discount" Schools

If a school offers a scholarship (price decrease), how much better does the outcome need to be to justify it?

**Example:**
- School A: $50,000/year (full price)
- School B: $40,000/year (20% discount)
- Your "contribution margin" = value you get from education

**Question:** Does the 20% cheaper school need to deliver proportionally more value?

**Answer:** It depends on what you're optimizing for:
- If optimizing for **career outcomes** (salary, job placement), the cheaper school needs to deliver comparable or better results
- If optimizing for **financial flexibility**, the savings compound over time
- If optimizing for **total life value**, consider non-financial factors

### College Pricing Strategy Insights

1. **Merit scholarships are price discrimination** - Schools charge different prices to different students based on perceived value/willingness to pay

2. **Tuition Exchange is a 100% discount** - Requires zero additional volume (you're already enrolled), making it incredibly valuable

3. **Employee benefits are strategic pricing** - Schools offer discounts to employees to attract talent and fill seats

4. **The "sticker price" is rarely the real price** - Most students pay less through aid/scholarships

5. **ROI matters more than price** - A $60K/year school with 98% job placement may be better value than a $40K/year school with 70% placement
