# MKTW Deep Dive - Why It's Cheap

**Market Cap:** $36M  
**Revenue (2025):** $328M  
**Revenue/Cap Ratio:** 9.1x

## The Answer: Declining Revenue

**3-Year Revenue Trend:**
- 2023: $448M
- 2024: $409M (-9%)
- 2025: $328M (-20%)

**MKTW is losing 20% revenue per year.**

## What Is MKTW?

- **Business:** Financial newsletter/subscription publisher
- **Former Name:** Ascendant Digital Acquisition Corp (SPAC merger 2021)
- **Products:** Investment research subscriptions
- **Model:** Subscription revenue

## Why Revenue Is Collapsing

From 10-K analysis:
1. **Subscriber churn** - Losing customers faster than acquiring
2. **Marketing costs** - High customer acquisition costs eating margins
3. **Reorganization** - Eliminated 2 of 3 operating subsidiaries in July 2025
4. **Competitive pressure** - Financial newsletter market is crowded

## The Numbers

**Income Statement (2025):**
- Revenue: $328M
- Income before tax: $67M
- Net margin: ~20%

**Still profitable!** But shrinking fast.

## Gary's Verdict: PASS

**Why it's cheap:**
- Revenue declining 20%/year = death spiral
- Market is pricing in continued decline
- At current trajectory: $262M revenue in 2026, $210M in 2027
- Eventually hits zero

**Why it's not a buy:**
- No turnaround catalyst visible
- Reorganization suggests desperation, not strategy
- SPAC mergers often overpromise, underdeliver
- Financial newsletter business is commoditizing

**The 10x revenue/cap ratio is a value trap, not a value opportunity.**
