## BRAYDEN'S CHECKLIST ✓

**Your Mission:** Build Evan's confidence, not just build walls.

**Tool Training Phase (Hour 1):**
- [ ] Start with easiest tool first (drill)
- [ ] Demonstrate on scrap, then let Evan try immediately
- [ ] Stand beside him, not behind (less intimidating)
- [ ] Say "That's it" when he does it right
- [ ] If mistake: "Let's try that again" (never "No, wrong")
- [ ] Let him practice until comfortable, not just once

**Teaching Checklist:**
- [ ] Show, don't just tell
- [ ] Let him make small mistakes (learning opportunity)
- [ ] Ask "What do you think we should do next?" (build ownership)
- [ ] Praise effort, not just results: "Good focus on that cut"
- [ ] If he's struggling: "This part's tricky, took me forever to learn"

**Frustration Warning Signs:**
- [ ] Evan goes quiet
- [ ] Rushing through steps
- [ ] Asking "Are we done yet?"
- [ ] Blaming tools or materials

**Your Response:**
- [ ] Slow down, break task into smaller steps
- [ ] Switch to different task for variety
- [ ] "Let's take a 5-minute break"
- [ ] Share your own learning struggles: "I messed this up so many times"

**Every 30 Minutes:**
- [ ] Give specific positive feedback: "That screw is perfectly flush"
- [ ] Check: Does Evan look engaged or checked out?
- [ ] Adjust pace if needed (slower is fine)

**Remember:**
- [ ] You're teaching a skill, not completing a job
- [ ] Evan's success = your success as a leader
- [ ] Patient teaching now = better helper later
